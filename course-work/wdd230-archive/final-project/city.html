About Salt Lake City

Salt Lake City is the capital city of the state of Utah. The city was settled by members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints in July of 1847, however, the Native American Tribes had long been present. Some of the nearby tribes included the Shoshone, Paiute, and Goshute people. Salt Lake City was nicknamed "the crossroads of the West" and it is known as the banking center of the United States of America. The 2002 Olympics were held in the state, and the surrounding area is famous for its diverse outdoor activities, the most popular being skiing. Salt Lake City also resides nearby most of the state's National Parks, such as Zion National Park and Arches National Park. The area is known for its proximity to the Great Salt Lake, and salt flats are scattered around the state.
According to the US Census Bureau in 2019, Salt Lake City's population consists of 72.8% white people, 21.8% Hispanic or Latino people, and 2.6% black or African American people. Salt Lake City has a wide range of weather conditions throughout the year, and the Great Salt Lake contributes to humidity. It snows a lot in the winter, making the nearby ski resorts very popular and well-known attractions.

Fun Facts!

  • The world headquarters of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints.
  • The area is often regarded as having the "best snow" in the world. This is based on its low moisture.
  • Salt Lake City was rated "gayest city" in America in 2012, and it hosts one of the largest gay pride parades in America.
  • With over 4.5 billion tons of salt in the Great Salt Lake, swimmers can float. You still need to know how to swim though.
  • The Great Salt Lake is home to the famous Spiral Jetty. Robert Smithson constructed this piece of nature art in 1970.
  • The city is proud to host a portion of the Sun Dance Film Festival.
  • The famous ski resorts around the city get an average snowfall of 500 inches per winter.
